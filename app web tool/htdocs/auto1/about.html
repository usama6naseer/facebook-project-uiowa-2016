  • About

The study aims to understand and expose the activity of malicious entities that generate fake activity on online social networks using a system of willing users. We call such entities collusion networks because they are made up of a network of users who mutually benefit from this system by contributing to each other’s influence on the social network in the form of likes, comments, followers, etc. These networks leverage the existing application infrastructure of the social networks. Apps provide some functionality to users (e.g., games or utility), which sometimes requires users to permit apps read/write access to their profiles. Collusion networks exploit app permissions to collaborate user activity. Users install an app, which is authorized to do activities (e.g., like a page, follow a user, comment on a picture) on their behalf without requiring account login credentials. The process is quid pro quo, users who enroll these services get likes/comments on their pages/posts, and their accounts are used to like/comment on other users’ pages/posts.
